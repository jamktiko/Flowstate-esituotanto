## Reporting, Communication, and Documentation

### Reporting

The team reports to the Project Manager every Friday at 9:15 AM during the weekly meeting.

### Time Tracking

Each team member tracks their own hours and reports them daily in the working hours tracking document.

### Meeting Practices

The weekly meeting is held every Friday at 9:15 AM at JAMK Rajakatu Campus. Scrum master records the meeting and reports its duration to all team

### Communication Methods

The project’s primary official internal communication channel is Microsoft Teams (chat and channel)

### Documentation

Each week, the team’s weekly meeting is documented in a shared file. The documentation includes progress from the previous week, plans for the upcoming week, issues encountered, and whether the project is staying on schedule.

### Document Management

The document is stored in the OneDrive cloud service. All team members have read access, but only the Scrum Master has write access. Versioning is handled internally within the document by dating each set of meeting notes.

## Risk Management

| Risks                                                 | Probability | Impact  | Mitigation                                                                                                       |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | ----------- | ------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Team members lose motivation or engagement            | Medium      | High    | Maintain a healthy team culture, keep workloads balanced, and address concerns early in weekly meetings.         |
| A team member becomes ill or unavailable              | Low         | High    | Share responsibilities, document work continuously, and assign backups for critical tasks.                       |
| Cloud service outage (for example, AWS downtime)      | Very Low    | Extreme | pray for besos                                                                                                   |
| Project schedule slips due to underestimated tasks    | Medium      | High    | Break work into smaller tasks, track progress weekly, and re-prioritize backlog items when delays occur.         |
| Critical bug is discovered late in development        | Medium      | High    | Use regular testing, code reviews, and early integration testing in each sprint.                                 |
| Key technical component is more complex than expected | Medium      | Medium  | Build early proof-of-concepts, reserve buffer time, and seek support from the Cloud Architect when needed.       |
| Communication gaps within the team                    | Medium      | Medium  | Use Microsoft Teams as the official channel, summarize decisions in writing, and confirm task ownership clearly. |
| Data loss or document version conflicts               | Low         | High    | Store files in OneDrive, follow naming and version conventions, and verify backups regularly.                    |
| Scope creep from adding extra features                | Medium      | Medium  | Keep a prioritized backlog, require team approval for scope changes, and focus on MVP-first delivery.            |
